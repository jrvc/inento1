ABOUT ME

I'm a first year PhD student working at the University of Helsinki under the supervision of Jörg Tiedemann and Mathias Creutz. I focus on the development of models for natural language understanding (NLU) with a data-driven approach that incorporates massively multilingual parallel corpora and the use of multimodal input signals. My research is centered on the development and implementation of multilingual and multimodal machine translation models and on the analysis of internal dynamics of such systems.

I received my MSc in Mathematical Modelling from Universitá degli Studi dell'Aquila in 2017 and his BSc in Applied Mathematics from ITAM (Mexico Autonomous Institute of Technology) in 2015. My research interests cosist on Advanced Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing and Applied Mathematics.
